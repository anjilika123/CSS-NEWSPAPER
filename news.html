'3 months too long to wait before cutting fuel taxes, Lacson says

By Javier Joe Ismael and Franco Jose C. Baroña | March 15, 2026

Panfilo Lacson is urging amendments to the country’s tax laws so the government can quickly suspend fuel excise taxes as global oil prices rise.

He noted that a safeguard in the Tax Reform for Acceleration and Inclusion Law (TRAIN Law)—which allowed the suspension of fuel excise tax hikes when oil prices hit a certain level—expired in 2020.

Under Article 148 of the National Internal Revenue Code of the Philippines, excise tax increases on petroleum products could be suspended from 2018 to 2020 if Dubai crude averaged $80 per barrel or higher for three months before the scheduled hike. That provision is no longer in effect.

PH firm on economic reforms, Marcos tells IMF

By Philippine News Agency | March 15, 2026

Ferdinand Marcos Jr. said the Philippines will continue pursuing reforms and sound economic policies to maintain stability and resilience despite global uncertainties.

In a Facebook post, Marcos shared details of his meeting with Kristalina Georgieva, managing director of the International Monetary Fund, where they discussed the country’s economic outlook and reforms to sustain growth.
